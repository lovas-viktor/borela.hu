§ 07 — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Common buyer questions

  • Do you handle heat treatment in-house?

    No — heat treatment is performed by a vetted, certified professional partner. However, Borela coordinates the entire workflow: scheduling, transport, parameters, and incoming inspection are all managed by us. You sign one contract and receive treated, inspected parts.

  • What is the typical lead time from RFQ to first sample?

    Quotation is returned within 48 hours of receiving the technical drawing. First sample typically follows in 2–4 weeks depending on complexity, material availability, and whether heat treatment is required (heat treatment adds approximately 1–2 weeks).

  • Can you handle high-mix low-volume orders alongside series production?

    Yes. Typical orders run from 500 to 100,000 pieces; we also produce smaller validation batches and prototype runs. The production planning is designed to keep both series volumes and prototype lots flowing without disruption.

  • What file formats do you accept for quoting?

    For 3D geometry: STEP (.stp / .step) and IGES (.igs / .iges). For 2D drawings: PDF and DXF. We can also work from native CAD files where required. Tolerances and surface finishes should be clearly indicated on the 2D drawing or in a separate spec sheet.

  • Do you sign NDAs before quoting?

    Yes. We routinely sign mutual NDAs before reviewing drawings, and we work under blue-chip-grade confidentiality controls — automotive and brake-systems customers have audited and approved our document handling. Send us your NDA or request our template.
